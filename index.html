Instrukcja

Zacznij od A1–A2. Odczytaj tekst wyraźnie i sprawdź rozumienie. Po ocenie system podpowie, czy iść wyżej.

A1–A2
Prosty dialog — gdzie jest kot?
Tom & Anna · słownictwo codzienne
— brak▾
📢 Tekst do odczytania
Tom: Hi, Anna! Where is your cat?
Anna: Oh, my cat is not at home. He is in the park with my brother.
Tom: Great! I will go and see them.
❓ Pytanie i odpowiedź
Where is the cat? (At home / At school / At the park)
✓ At the park
Ocena
B1
Dialog tematyczny — wycieczka w góry
Mary & John · szczegóły planu
— brak▾
📢 Tekst
Mary: Hi, John! Are you ready for the trip tomorrow?
John: Yes, I can't wait! We're going to the mountains with my classmates.
Mary: How long are you staying there?
John: Just for three days – from Friday to Sunday. We're going hiking, and on Saturday evening we're going to have a barbecue by the river.
Mary: Are you staying in a hotel?
John: No, we're renting a small house near the forest. It's cheaper and much more fun.
❓ Pytania
Where are they going?
✓ The mountains
How many days?
✓ Three days
What on Saturday evening?
✓ Barbecue by the river
Where are they staying?
✓ Small house near the forest

B2
Dialog z opiniami — Włochy latem
Alex & Sarah · opinie, preferencje
— brak▾
📢 Tekst
Alex: I heard you're travelling this summer. Where are you going?
Sarah: I'm flying to Italy — Rome, Florence, and Venice.
Alex: How long will you stay?
Sarah: About ten days. We'll see tourist attractions but also spend time in smaller towns.
Alex: Do you prefer summer or winter travel?
Sarah: Definitely summer — I love warm weather and walking around cities. I'd rather lie on a beach than ski in the cold.
Alex: How are you travelling between cities?
Sarah: Mostly by train — fast, comfortable, and great views.
❓ Pytania
Which cities?
✓ Rome, Florence, Venice
How long?
✓ 10 days
Why summer travel?
✓ Warm weather, walking cities
How between cities?
✓ By train

C1
Wypowiedź formalna — technologia i edukacja
Monolog radiowy · abstrakcja, argumenty
— brak▾
📢 Tekst
In today's programme, we focus on the impact of technology on education. Online platforms have become extremely popular, offering flexibility and low cost. Many learners appreciate studying at their own pace. However, experts argue that digital tools cannot replace face-to-face interaction — human contact is essential for confidence and motivation. The challenge for the future is finding a balance: combining the flexibility of online tools with the personal support of a teacher.
❓ Pytania
Advantages of online learning?
✓ Flexibility, low cost, self-paced
Why criticised?
✓ Cannot replace human interaction
What is the future challenge?
✓ Balance tools + personal teacher support
Ocena
C2
Wykład akademicki — globalizacja i tożsamość
Monolog złożony · wiele perspektyw, wnioski
— brak▾
📢 Tekst
Globalisation has made the world increasingly interconnected: access to international markets, cultural exchange, faster technological development. Yet it has created challenges — local traditions and languages are disappearing, communities feel their identity is under threat. English dominates business, which helps communication but may crowd out smaller languages. Some experts see this as inevitable and beneficial; others argue cultural diversity is essential. The key question: how do we enjoy globalisation's benefits without destroying what makes each culture unique? Perhaps education — teaching young people to value both global cooperation and local traditions — holds the answer.
❓ Pytania
Advantages of globalisation?
✓ Markets, cultural exchange, tech
Dangers?
✓ Loss of traditions, languages dying
Expert disagreement?
✓ Some beneficial, others harmful
Possible solution?
✓ Education valuing cooperation + local traditions

Samoopis, czas teraźniejszy
— brak▾

1
Can you tell me something about yourself?
Spróbuj: "How old are you?" · "Where do you live?" · "Do you have a pet?" · "What's your name?"

Do you have any brothers or sisters?

+1
What's your favourite colour / food / animal?

"Is it a dog or cat?" · "What's its name?" · "Is it big or small?"
+3
Can you describe your room or house?
"Is it big or small?" · "What colour are the walls?" · "What's in your room?"
+4
What do you do on Sundays?
"Do you stay at home?" · "Do you go out?" · "Do you eat with family?"

Who is your best friend? Tell me about them.

🗣 Pytania główne
1
What is your biggest ambition in life — the one thing you really want to achieve?
Spróbuj: "Professional or personal ambition?" · "Something you've always wanted to do?" · "By when do you want to achieve it?"
2
If you were given a chance, would you live your life all over again?
Spróbuj: "Is there something you'd change?" · "What decision was most important?" · "Do you have regrets?"
3
What would you do with $1,000,000?
Spróbuj: "Invest, donate, travel, or something else?" · "Would you tell anyone?" · "Would it change you as a person?"
4
If stranded on a desert island — what one thing would you take with you?
Spróbuj: "Practical or emotional choice?" · "Would you want to be rescued?" · "What would you miss most?"
5
Does happiness depend more on success or on relationships?
Spróbuj: "Think of the happiest people you know." · "Can you be successful and unhappy?" · "What does success even mean to you?"
6
Should governments actively protect minority languages? Why / why not?
Spróbuj: "Think of Welsh, Irish, Catalan." · "Is it worth spending money on?" · "What do we lose when a language dies?"
📦 Pytania zapasowe C1/C2
+1
Is social media making us more or less connected as people?
"Online vs offline connection." · "Does it depend on how you use it?"
+2
Should schools teach financial literacy as a core subject?
"Did you learn about money at school?" · "What would you include in such a course?"
+3
What does success mean to you personally — and has it changed over time?
"When did you feel most successful?" · "Do other people's opinions matter?"
+4
Is it possible to be truly objective about anything?
"Think: news, science, personal opinions." · "Can you give an example?"
+5
What responsibility do wealthy countries have towards poorer ones?
"Aid, trade, migration, climate." · "Is it charity or obligation?"
+6
Should animals have legal rights similar to humans?
"Which animals?" · "What rights specifically?" · "Where do you draw the line?"
+7
Is it ever morally acceptable to lie?
"White lies?" · "Lying to protect someone?" · "Does intention matter?"
+8
If you could change one thing about the world, what would it be and why?
"Big or small change?" · "Would it have unintended consequences?"
📊 Klucz
C1
My ambition is to become a doctor. I would live my life again. With $1M I would study, donate and travel.
C2
I'd take a satellite phone. Happiness is inherently relational. Governments have a moral duty to preserve linguistic heritage through education.
C1: Płynny i spontaniczny, precyzyjnie wyraża złożone poglądy, bogaty zasób. C2: Swobodny, elastyczny, subtelne niuanse, humor i ironia, niemal jak native speaker.
